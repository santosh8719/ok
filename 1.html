‚ö° Chapter 1: Electric Charges and Fields (Physics)

Electric charge is a fundamental property of matter. It can be positive or negative. Like charges repel, unlike charges attract each other.

  • Coulomb‚Äôs Law: Force between two charges is proportional to product of charges and inversely proportional to square of distance.
  • Electric Field: Space around a charge where force is experienced.
  • Electric Dipole: Two equal and opposite charges separated by distance.

Applications: Capacitors, Van de Graaff Generator, Lightning Rods.

üß™ Chapter 2: Solid State (Chemistry)

In solids, particles are closely packed. Solids have definite mass, volume, and shape.

  • Classification: Crystalline and Amorphous solids.
  • Unit Cell: Smallest repeating unit in crystal lattice.
  • Types of Cubic Unit Cells: Simple Cubic, Body-Centered Cubic (BCC), Face-Centered Cubic (FCC).

Important Defects: Schottky and Frenkel Defects (important for NEET).

üß¨ Chapter 3: Human Reproduction (Biology)

Reproduction is the biological process by which new individuals are produced from their parents.

  • Male Reproductive System: Testes, Vas deferens, Urethra, Penis.
  • Female Reproductive System: Ovaries, Fallopian tubes, Uterus, Vagina.
  • Fertilization occurs in the fallopian tube.

NEET Focus: Menstrual cycle stages, hormonal control, implantation process.
